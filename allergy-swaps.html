Swapping Ingredients in Classic Dishes for Allergy-Friendly Options

Food allergies should not be a sentence to bland eating. With the right swaps, you can recreate the texture and flavor of almost any classic dish.

Gluten: The Binder

Gluten provides structure. When removing it (e.g., swapping flour for almond meal), you lose elasticity. To combat this, use binders like Xanthan Gum or Psyllium Husk. For thickening sauces, Cornstarch or Arrowroot are superior colorless options.

Dairy: The Fat and The Tang

Replacing milk is easy; replacing buttermilk or heavy cream requires more thought.
Tip: Coconut cream mimics the mouthfeel of heavy cream. For buttermilk's tang, add a teaspoon of vinegar to soy milk and let it sit for 5 minutes.

Nuts: The Crunch

Seeds are your best friend. Roasted sunflower seeds or pumpkin seeds (pepitas) offer the same fatty crunch as pine nuts in pesto or walnuts in a salad, completely nut-free.
