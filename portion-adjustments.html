Portion Adjustments: Scaling Recipes Up and Down Like a Pro

Cooking for a dinner party versus cooking for yourself requires more than just multiplication. It requires an understanding of physics, chemistry, and equipment.

The Science of Scaling

When you double a recipe, you don't necessarily double the cooking time. Surface area affects evaporation rates. A stew cooked in a small pot will reduce differently than a double batch in a large Dutch oven.

Spices and Leavening

Spices do not always scale linearly. Doubling the chili powder might make a dish aggressively spicy rather than just "more" spicy. Start with 1.5x and taste. Similarly, baking powder has diminishing returns in large batches.
